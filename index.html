Research Interest

My research focuses on exploring different stellar feedback mechanisms and how they shape the evolution of the interstellar medium. I'm particularly interested in the Gum Nebula, a region in our Milky Way, and various stellar activities occurring within it. I aim to build a comprehensive 3D picture of dense gas and young stars to describe their distribution and dynamics.

Current Project

I am currently working on constraining the geometry of the IRAS Vela Shell (IVS), a cavity in the interstellar medium in our solar neighborhood.

Using a new-generation 3D dust map created with the measurements of stellar extinction (aka, the amount of stellar light that is absorbed and scattered by interstellar dust and gas during propagation), we are able to study the IVS structure in 3D. Combined with the shell dynamic information, we can further look into the origin of this strucure, which helps us understand how different types of stellar feedback (stellar wind, supernova activities, UV radiation, etc) collectively contribute to the morphology of the interstellar medium.

The structure of the IRAS Vela Shell, together with stellar sources in its vicinity are presented in this interactive plot. In this project, we also traced the movement of these main objects in the frame of a star cluster family 'gamma Vel' family, first recognized in Swiggum et al. 2024.. We find that in the gamma Vel family, these star clusters seems to trace back to a common center, and the IRAS Vela Shell is also located in this region. The interactive figure for these clusters in the frame of gamma Vel family can be found here. It is different than the clusters we selected in the general LSR frame, where a different group of clusters might be causing the IVS to expand, as shown here. Stay tuned as the paper is on the way!
